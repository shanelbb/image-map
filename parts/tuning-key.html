The reason your instrument has tuning keys in the first place is to give you the ability to easily adjust string tension. A single tuning peg consists of a cylinder that sits in a pinion gear with a worm drive connecting them. By threading the string through the cylinder, you can tighten or loosen it by turning the tuning knob.
